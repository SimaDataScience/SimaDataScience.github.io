First Steps in Machine Learning

Rough Draft Machine Learning Models

Data scientists are well aware that creating an effective and production-ready model takes time, experimentation, and a lot of attention to detail. However, there are many times when a scalable model isn't necessary, and only a rough first draft is needed. The most common time this is done is as a first step in creating a more robust and scalable model: it's a good idea to start with a simple pipeline and simple model to give you a sense of how difficult the prediciton task is. And who knows, this simple model may even reach your performance goals already if you're lucky and the task is simple! However, Occam's razor isn't the only driving force towards simplicity: time is often a very real factor, and this also requires preference of simplicity. I recently encountered a situation that required me to create a "rough draft" model in a very short timeframe, and as I feel the process was very illustrative of how I like to approach similar problems, I would like to share the process with you.

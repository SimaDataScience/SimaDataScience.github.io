Image Regression in Kaggle's Pawpularity Contest

Regression with CNNs

I found an interesting Kaggle competition that asks you to predict an image's score, or 'Pawpularity'. My first thought was to use a pretrained network, but I decided to try training some convolutional neural nets from scratch first. I did ultimately use a pretrained network for this competition, but the lessons learned by starting with my own simple models ultimately led to my success later in this competition and was well worth the time. I'm a big believer in taking the crawl/walk/run approach to projects, and that finding the ideal approach to a problem is often accomplished by starting with something simple.
